Understanding LSTM Neural Networks

Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks are a special kind of recurrent neural network (RNN) capable of learning long-term dependencies. They were introduced by Hochreiter & Schmidhuber in 1997 and have become one of the most popular architectures for sequence prediction problems.

LSTMs are particularly useful in tasks such as speech recognition, language modeling, machine translation, and time series forecasting, where understanding context over long sequences is crucial.
